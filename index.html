Some good reads

The Truce by Primo Levi

The author's odyssey through Europe, in the attempt of reaching his home in the wake of WW2.

Moby Dick by Herman Melville

Sailor Ishmael's narrative of the obsessive quest of Ahab, captain of the whaling ship Pequod, for revenge on Moby Dick, the white whale that on the ship's previous voyage bit off Ahab's leg at the knee.

The Moon and The Bonfires by Cesare Pavese

The protagonist has returned to his home town in the years immediately following the Second World War. He left twenty-five years earlier and had made his fortune in the United States. Returning to his home town, he finds many of the same smells and sights that filled his youth, but he also finds a town and its inhabitants that have been deeply changed by war and by the passage of time.
